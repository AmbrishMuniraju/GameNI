# 02 — Target Market & Personas

## Geographic Strategy: Density-First

### Phase 1: Belfast City (Win One Postcode Completely)
Most platforms launch citywide and stay shallow. We will **monopolize a single corridor** before expanding.

**Target Zone:** South Belfast → East Belfast corridor
- Botanic / Queen's Quarter (student + young professional density)
- Ormeau / Lower Ormeau (residential, family-heavy, central)
- East Belfast / Avoniel (established sports infrastructure)
- Castlereagh (leisure centre density)

**Why this works:** Liquidity is non-linear. A user with 8 game options at 6 PM converts. A user with 1 option does not. Depth in one neighbourhood beats breadth across ten.

**Target:** 500+ active weekly players in this 3-square-mile zone before any expansion.

### Phase 2: Northern Ireland Expansion
- Derry / Londonderry
- Lisburn
- Newry
- Antrim / Ballymena

### Phase 3: Cross-Border & UK
- Dublin (massive market, similar cultural dynamics)
- Manchester, Liverpool, Glasgow (UK cities with strong community sport culture)

## Core Personas

### Persona 1: "The Transplant" — Maya, 29

**Profile:** Moved to Belfast for a tech/finance role. Played university sport. Has the gear, the money, and 4-6 free evenings. Follows her old hometown team on Instagram.

**Stated Need:** "I just want to find a regular pickup game."

**Silent Pain Points:**
- She is grieving the loss of an athletic identity she took for granted
- She reframes loneliness as "being busy with work"
- She fears that walking into a park alone, at her age, looks pathetic
- She doesn't want a one-time match — she wants **a Tuesday**. She is shopping for **ritual**, not recreation
- She suspects her body has aged faster than her self-image, and dreads finding out in front of strangers

**What Pitch Does For Her:**
- The Lurk Layer lets her browse and watch before committing
- "Soft Arrival" chat lets her message the group before showing up
- She sees the host's rating and the skill level — reducing the unknown
- After 3 sessions, she becomes a "Regular" at that venue — she has her Tuesday

---

### Persona 2: "The Inconsistent Intermediate" — David, 36

**Profile:** Senior PM with two kids. Genuinely loves the game. Was a B-grade club player. Now has a chaotic calendar — travel, kids' bedtime, last-minute work. Owns three rackets and uses none.

**Stated Need:** "I just need flexible drop-in games."

**Silent Pain Points:**
- He has internalised a self-image as a flaker, and every cancelled commitment confirms it
- He resents leagues for punishing the life stage in which he most needs social contact
- He doesn't want a team — he wants a **dependable supply of games he can opt into 4 hours in advance**
- He is willing to pay a significant premium for commitment-free reliability
- He secretly worries that if he stops playing entirely, a part of his identity will quietly die

**What Pitch Does For Him:**
- No commitment required — browse games, join 4 hours before, or sub-in when someone drops
- "Join as Reserve" means he can signal intent without the guilt of flaking
- No-shame cancellation — his spot is auto-filled, no drama
- Premium drop-in option: £5 extra for guaranteed last-minute slots

> **This is the most commercially attractive segment.** High disposable income, high willingness-to-pay, and every existing format actively punishes them.

### Persona 3: "The Organizer-Burnout" — Priya, 41

**Profile:** For five years, she has been the one who books the court, chases Monzo/Revolut payments, herds the group, and absorbs the no-shows. The group exists because she exists.

**Stated Need:** "I just want someone else to handle the logistics."

**Silent Pain Points:**
- She suspects her friendships are transactional — people only show up when she plans
- She wants to step back without being responsible for the group's death
- She resents being cast as the "mum" of a group of adults
- She would pay £20/month to never chase money again; she would pay £200/month to outsource leadership without losing status

**What Pitch Does For Her:**
- Auto-payment splitting (everyone pays before the game, no chasing)
- Waitlist management (reserves auto-fill if someone drops)
- "Trusted Host" badge and public profile — her status is preserved and amplified
- Revenue share on bookings she generates
- She is not a user. She is **infrastructure**.

> **Acquiring 1 organizer = acquiring 15-50 players.** CAC drops by an order of magnitude.
