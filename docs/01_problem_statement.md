# 01 — Problem Statement & Solution

## The Problem Space

### Surface-Level Problem
> "There's nowhere to book a pitch and no one to play with."

This is what users *say*. It is not the real problem. Belfast has 50+ bookable sports facilities. The real problem is invisible.

### The Actual Problem: Psychological Friction
Technology has solved logistics (you can book a court online) but has *relocated* friction from the logistical layer to the psychological layer:

| Friction Type | What It Feels Like | Who It Blocks |
|---|---|---|
| **Trust Friction** | "Will these strangers show up? Will they be good? Will they be aggressive?" | Everyone new |
| **Commitment Friction** | "I can't commit to every Tuesday for 12 weeks." | Busy professionals, parents |
| **Social Arrival Friction** | "Walking into a park alone at 30 feels pathetic." | Transplants, introverts |
| **Organizer Burden** | "I'm tired of being the one who books, chases, and absorbs no-shows." | Group admins |
| **Skill Anxiety** | "What if I'm terrible and everyone notices?" | Returning athletes |
| **Identity Friction** | "If I stop playing, a part of who I am quietly dies." | Lapsed athletes |

### The 4 Dimensions of Vulnerability
When you play sport with strangers, you expose four things simultaneously:
1. **Your physical body** — out of breath, sweating, aging
2. **Your skill level** — the ego risk
3. **Your competitive temperament** — do you argue calls? Sulk? Quit?
4. **Your social fluency** — locker room banter, post-game dynamics

Most platforms only solve #2 (skill matching) and assume the rest follows. They don't.

### Why Existing Solutions Fail

| Format | Barrier | Retention | Social Density | Hidden Failure |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Formal Leagues** | High (fees, fixed nights, season commitment) | High (sunk-cost) | Low (same 8 people) | Punishes the busy |
| **WhatsApp Groups** | Very High (invite-only, tribal) | Very High (social glue) | High internally, zero permeability | Cliques calcify; outsiders can't enter |
| **Public Park Casuals** | Very Low (just show up) | Very Low (no continuity) | Low (different faces weekly) | Quality is unpredictable |

> **The gap:** No format combines **Low Barrier + Medium Retention + Medium Social Density**. That diagonal is the entire market opportunity.

## The Solution: Pitch

### Core Thesis
> The winning platform won't optimize for *transactional liquidity* (matching supply to demand at time T). It will optimize for **psychological liquidity** — lowering the cost of trying, the cost of flaking, and the cost of leading.

### Three Strategic Pillars

#### Pillar 1: Graduated Commitment
Instead of forcing a binary (commit publicly or don't exist), Pitch offers a funnel:
```
Lurk → Watch → Signal Interest → Sub-In → Join → Become a Regular → Host
```
Each step has lower psychological cost than any existing platform demands.

#### Pillar 2: Organizer-as-Infrastructure
The person who books the court, chases the money, and absorbs the no-shows is our **most valuable user**. We don't just serve them — we *hire* them. We give them tools, revenue share, and social status.

#### Pillar 3: Absorb Inconsistency
Instead of punishing flakes (like leagues do), we **price, manage, and absorb** human inconsistency:
- AI-managed reserves auto-fill empty spots
- Late cancellations trigger the waitlist, not a penalty
- "Shows Up" reliability scores reward consistency without punishing life

### How Technology Should Work
> **Key Principle:** Technology should make vulnerability cheaper, not more visible.

- Elo ratings should be **invisible** (the system matches you; you never see a number)
- Post-game feedback should be **about the game**, not about rating individuals
- The first session should have a **free "observer" option** — watch from the bench, buy a drink, meet the group
